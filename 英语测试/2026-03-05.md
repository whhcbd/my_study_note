## 听力部分

### 原文（来源：VOA Learning English, 2026年6月英语四级听力样题）

The future has arrived at a dairy farm about in an hour by car from Washington D.C. A robot is milking cows. It is milking time at Woodbourne Creamery in Mt. Airy, Maryland. The cows are standing in line for a turn at the robot.

John Fendrick is the owner of the farm. He inspects the progress of the animals by looking at a computer screen. That is all he has to do.

"The door of the milk opens up, they walk in, they get milked. The door opens up, they walk out."

John Fendrick checks the progress on the screen.

The robot does all the work. It uses a laser to find each teat -- the place on the cow where the milk comes out. The robot then cleans the teat and connects a milking tube to it.

The robot also tests the milk for possible contamination. If it finds a problem, it rejects the milk. When the amount of milk coming out of the cow slows, the machine knows to stop, and sends the cow on its way.

Milking robots are becoming popular among dairy farmers in the United States, Europe and Australia. John Fendrick says the robots have changed life on a dairy farm.

"You are giving the freedom back. So it allows me or the people work for me to actually do other things on the farm."

Most dairy farmers must milk their cows two times a day, early in the morning and late at night. The animals are milked everyday in good weather or bad. But Mr Fendrick's cows do not follow such schedule, they stay in the field until they want to be milked by the robot. Some of them come to be milked in the middle of the night.

Mr Fendrick doesn't even need to be there to watch his cows be milked. He can watch from his telephone. He can learn when each cow was milked and how much she produced. He can also learn if a cow has not been milked for a long time.

The cow milking robot is not low cost technology. Mr Fendrick paid more than $150,000 for it. But he notes, paying someone to milk the cows is also costly.

"In three years, I will have paid off the difference with this, and I don't have to be the person who's always on call to milk. The fact that we have a life, and our cows are able to function without us -- to us, it's well worth the money."

And he says that is a good thing. "I don't like to milk." With the robot, he can turn his attention to other things. He gets about 475 liters of milk a day, and he never has to touch a cow.

And that's the Agriculture Report from VOA Learning English. For more agriculture stories, go to our website 51voa.com. I'm Caty Weaver.

### 题目（共5题，每题1分）

**1. What does the robot use to locate the cow's teat?**
A. A camera
B. A laser
C. A sensor
D. A manual operator

**2. How does the robot detect possible contamination in the milk?**
A. By checking the color
B. By testing the milk
C. By measuring the temperature
D. By smelling the milk

**3. Why does Mr. Fendrick think the robot is worth the money?**
A. It increases milk production significantly.
B. It reduces labor costs and gives him more freedom.
C. It makes the cows happier and healthier.
D. It attracts tourists to the farm.

**4. How many times a day do traditional dairy farmers typically milk their cows?**
A. Once
B. Twice
C. Three times
D. Whenever the cows want

**5. What is the main impact of the robot on the farmer's lifestyle?**
A. It requires more technical skills.
B. It allows the farmer to monitor the cows remotely and have more free time.
C. It forces the farmer to stay in the barn all day.
D. It increases the farmer's daily workload.

### 答案与解析

**1. B** 细节题。原文明确提到 "It uses a laser to find each teat"。因此正确答案是B。

**2. B** 细节题。原文指出 "The robot also tests the milk for possible contamination." 故答案为B。

**3. B** 推理题。Mr. Fendrick 提到机器人让他不必随时待命挤奶，可以拥有自己的生活，并且从成本角度三年内就能回本。这体现了节省劳动成本和获得自由。因此B选项最全面。

**4. B** 细节题。原文说 "Most dairy farmers must milk their cows two times a day, early in the morning and late at night." 故答案为B。

**5. B** 推理题。从原文中 Mr. Fendrick 可以电话监控、不必亲临现场、可以做其他事情等描述，可以推断机器人带来了远程监控和更多的自由时间。因此B正确。

### 听力技巧提示
- 关注技术类说明文中的**动词+名词**搭配，如 "use a laser", "test the milk", "pay off the difference"。
- 注意数字和频率信息，如 "$150,000", "two times a day", "three years"。
- 长对话或篇章中，说话者的**评价性语句**（如 "well worth the money"）往往是推理题的考点。

## 阅读部分

### 原文（来源：新东方在线，2026年6月英语四级阅读练习：仔细阅读（1））

It happens to every medical student sooner or later. You get a cough that persists for a while. Ordinarily,you would just ignore it--but now, armed with your rapidly growing medical knowledge, you can’t help worrying. The cough could mean just a cold, but it could also be a sign of lung cancer.

For doctors in training, nurses and medical journalists, hypochondria is an occupational danger. The feeling usually passes after a while, leaving only a funny story to tell at a dinner party. But for the tens ofthousands who suffer from true hypochondria they live in constant terror that they are dying of some awfuldisease, or even several awful diseases at once. Doctors can assure them that there’s nothing wrong, but since the cough is real, the assurances fall on deaf ears. And because no physician or test can offer a 100% guarantee that one doesn’t have cancer, a hypochondriac always has fuel to feed Iris .or her worst fears.

Hypochondriacs don't harm just themselves; they block the whole healthcare system. Although they account for only about 6% of the patients who visit doctors every year, they tend to burden their physicians with frequent visits that take up excessive amounts of time. And the problem may be worse, thanks to the popularity of medical information on the Internet. They go on the Web and learn about new diseases and new presentations of old diseases that they never even knew about before. Doctors have taken to calling this phenomenon cyberchondria (网络疑病症).

### 题目（共5题，每题1分）

1. According to the passage, if you suffer from hypochondria, ______.
   A. you must be a medical student, or a medical worker
   B. you are haunted by a possibly inexistent disease
   C. you will never get rid of this disease
   D. you always tell funny stories at dinner parties

2. Which of the following best summarizes the main idea of the passage?
   A. Hypochondria happens to everybody sooner or later.
   B. We needn’t worry about hypochondria since it is not dangerous at all.
   C. Hypochondria originates from too much knowledge of medicine.
   D. Not only individuals but also the healthcare system might be disturbed by unnecessary terrors.

3. Why can’t doctors convince the suffers that there is nothing wrong?
   A. Because the doctors can’t cure the minor diseases
   B. Because the doctors don’t assure them of that
   C. Because the sufferers are deaf and cannot hear what the doctors say
   D. Because lack of absolute guarantee makes the patients doubtful

4. The problem becomes worse due to _____
   A. the increasing number of patients
   B. the widespread medical knowledge on the Internet
   C. the patients,regular visits to doctors that occupy too much time
   D. new diseases and symptoms emerge constantly

5. What does the author most probably think about hypochondria?
   A. The author considers that hypochondria is an incurable disease
   B. The author thinks that the consequences of hypochondria might be disastrous
   C. The author suggests that the patients who have hypochondria should set their hearts at rest
   D. The author sympathizes with the patients who suffer from hypochondria

### 答案与解析

1. **B** 事实细节题。第2段指出疑病症患者持续恐惧自己患有严重疾病，即使医生保证没事也无济于事。因此B正确。
2. **D** 主旨大意题。文章先描述疑病症对个人的影响，后段指出其对医疗系统的负担，D选项全面概括。
3. **D** 事实细节题。文中明确提到“因为没有任何医生或检查能100%保证没有癌症”，所以患者始终怀疑。
4. **B** 推理判断题。第3段提到“多亏了互联网上医学信息的普及”，问题更加严重，故B正确。
5. **C** 观点态度题。作者认为疑病症源于心理多疑，并非绝症，患者应放宽心。因此C最符合作者观点。

### 阅读技巧提示
- 科普类文章常采用 **“现象—定义—影响”** 结构，抓住段落首尾句快速把握主旨。
- 注意**转折词**（but, however）和**因果关系词**（because, thanks to），其后往往是考点。
- 选项中**绝对化表述**（如never, must, all）通常为干扰项，需对照原文核实。

## 翻译部分

### 中文句子
随着人工智能技术的飞速发展，许多传统行业正面临着前所未有的变革与挑战。

### 参考译文
With the rapid development of artificial intelligence technology, many traditional industries are facing unprecedented transformation and challenges.

### 重点词汇/句型解析
- **人工智能技术**：artificial intelligence technology（固定搭配）
- **飞速发展**：rapid development（也可用breakthrough advancement）
- **面临着**：are facing（现在进行时，强调持续状态）
- **前所未有的**：unprecedented（高级形容词，替代never before seen）
- **变革与挑战**：transformation and challenges（并列名词，常见于论述文）

## 写作片段

### 写作话题
**观点对比型**：有些人认为大学生应该专注于专业课程，另一些人则认为广泛涉猎不同学科更重要。请阐述你的看法。

### 范文（约100词）
In my view, a balanced approach that combines depth in one's major with breadth across disciplines is ideal. Specialized knowledge equips students with core competencies for future careers, while interdisciplinary exposure fosters critical thinking and adaptability. For instance, an engineering student who studies psychology may better understand user‑centered design. Therefore, universities should encourage elective courses and cross‑disciplinary projects. Ultimately, the goal of higher education is not merely to produce experts in a narrow field, but to cultivate well‑rounded individuals capable of tackling complex real‑world problems.

### 写作要点分析
1. **结构清晰**：首句亮明观点，中间分述专业深度与跨学科广度的价值，最后总结。
2. **例证具体**：以“工科生学心理学”为例，增强说服力。
3. **词汇亮点**：balanced approach, core competencies, interdisciplinary exposure, critical thinking, well‑rounded individuals 等学术表达。
4. **句型多样**：使用并列句（while...）、举例句（For instance...）、结论句（Ultimately...）等。
