## 二、阅读部分

**短文来源**：2026年6月英语四级阅读练习：仔细阅读（1）——新东方在线

**短文原文**：
> It happens to every medical student sooner or later. You get a cough that persists for a while. Ordinarily,you would just ignore it--but now, armed with your rapidly growing medical knowledge, you can’t help worrying. The cough could mean just a cold, but it could also be a sign of lung cancer.
> 
> For doctors in training, nurses and medical journalists, hypochondria is an occupational danger. The feeling usually passes after a while, leaving only a funny story to tell at a dinner party. But for the tens of thousands who suffer from true hypochondria they live in constant terror that they are dying of some awful disease, or even several awful diseases at once. Doctors can assure them that there’s nothing wrong, but since the cough is real, the assurances fall on deaf ears. And because no physician or test can offer a 100% guarantee that one doesn’t have cancer, a hypochondriac always has fuel to feed Iris .or her worst fears.
> 
> Hypochondriacs don’t harm just themselves; they block the whole healthcare system. Although they account for only about 6% of the patients who visit doctors every year, they tend to burden their physicians with frequent visits that take up excessive amounts of time. And the problem may be worse, thanks to the popularity of medical information on the Internet. They go on the Web and learn about new diseases and new presentations of old diseases that they never even knew about before. Doctors have taken to calling this phenomenon cyberchondria (网络疑病症).

**全文翻译**：
> 这件事迟早会发生在每个医学生身上。你咳嗽持续了一段时间。通常你会忽略它——但现在，凭借你迅速增长的医学知识，你忍不住担心。咳嗽可能只是感冒，但也可能是肺癌的征兆。
> 
> 对于培训中的医生、护士和医学记者来说，疑病症是一种职业风险。这种感觉通常会在一段时间后消失，只留下一个在晚宴上讲述的有趣故事。但对于成千上万患有真正疑病症的人来说，他们生活在持续的恐惧中，害怕自己死于某种可怕的疾病，甚至同时患上几种可怕的疾病。医生可以保证他们没事，但由于咳嗽是真实的，这些保证充耳不闻。而且因为没有医生或检查能提供100%的保证说某人没有癌症，疑病症患者总是有燃料来助长他们最深的恐惧。
> 
> 疑病症患者不仅伤害自己；他们还阻塞了整个医疗系统。尽管他们只占每年就诊患者的约6%，但他们往往通过频繁就诊给医生带来负担，占用过多时间。而且这个问题可能更糟，这要归功于互联网上医学信息的普及。他们上网了解以前甚至不知道的新疾病和旧疾病的新表现。医生开始称这种现象为“网络疑病症”。

**练习题**：
1. According to the passage, if you suffer from hypochondria, ______.
   A. you must be a medical student, or a medical worker
   B. you are haunted by a possibly inexistent disease
   C. you will never get rid of this disease
   D. you always tell funny stories at dinner parties

2. Which of the following best summarizes the main idea of the passage?
   A. Hypochondria happens to everybody sooner or later.
   B. We needn’t worry about hypochondria since it is not dangerous at all.
   C. Hypochondria originates from too much knowledge of medicine.
   D. Not only individuals but also the healthcare system might be disturbed by unnecessary terrors.

3. Why can’t doctors convince the suffers that there is nothing wrong?
   A. Because the doctors can’t cure the minor diseases
   B. Because the doctors don’t assure them of that
   C. Because the sufferers are deaf and cannot hear what the doctors say
   D. Because lack of absolute guarantee makes the patients doubtful

4. The problem becomes worse due to _____
   A. the increasing number of patients
   B. the widespread medical knowledge on the Internet
   C. the patients,regular visits to doctors that occupy too much time
   D. new diseases and symptoms emerge constantly

5. What does the author most probably think about hypochondria?
   A. The author considers that hypochondria is an incurable disease
   B. The author thinks that the consequences of hypochondria might be disastrous
   C. The author suggests that the patients who have hypochondria should set their hearts at rest
   D. The author sympathizes with the patients who suffer from hypochondria

**答案与解析**：
1. **B** – 事实细节题。第2段说明疑病症患者被可能不存在的疾病所困扰。
2. **D** – 主旨大意题。全文从个人症状谈到对医疗系统的干扰，选项D最全面。
3. **D** – 事实细节题。原文指出因为没有100%的保证，患者仍会怀疑。
4. **B** – 推理判断题。第3段提到“thanks to the popularity of medical information on the Internet”。
5. **C** – 观点态度题。作者认为疑病症源于心理多疑，只要放宽心就能缓解。

**阅读技巧**：
- **主旨题定位**：注意首段引入、末段总结，以及反复出现的关键词（hypochondria, healthcare system）。
- **细节题同义替换**：如“fall on deaf ears”对应“assurances don’t work”，“fuel to feed fears”对应“reason to continue worrying”。
- **推理题依据原文**：避免过度推断，答案必须基于文中明确或隐含的信息。

## 三、翻译部分

**汉译英句子翻译题**：
> 尽管这个实验失败了，但我们从中获得了宝贵的数据，这些数据将为未来的研究提供关键线索。

**参考答案**：
> Although this experiment failed, we obtained valuable data from it, which will provide key clues for future research.

**句型分析**：
1. **让步状语从句**：“尽管……但……”用“Although…”引导，主句无需再加but。
2. **定语从句**：“这些数据”指代前面整个句子的内容，用非限制性定语从句“which…”连接，使句子更紧凑。
3. **动词搭配**：“获得数据”译为“obtain data”，“提供线索”译为“provide clues”，均为四级高频搭配。
4. **形容词选用**：“宝贵的”用“valuable”，“关键的”用“key”，避免重复使用important。

**常见考点延伸**：
- 若改为“如果实验成功，我们就能获得宝贵数据”，需使用虚拟语气：If the experiment had succeeded, we could have obtained valuable data.
- 若强调“正是这些数据提供了线索”，可用强调句：It is these data that provide key clues.

## 四、写作片段

**写作主题**：图表描述  
假设以下图表展示了某大学2019-2023年学生使用电子书与纸质书的比例变化，请撰写一段80词左右的段落描述主要趋势。

| 年份 | 电子书比例 | 纸质书比例 |
|------|------------|------------|
| 2019 | 30%        | 70%        |
| 2020 | 45%        | 55%        |
| 2021 | 60%        | 40%        |
| 2022 | 75%        | 25%        |
| 2023 | 85%        | 15%        |

**范文**：
> The chart illustrates the shifting preference between e-books and printed books among university students from 2019 to 2023. In 2019, printed books accounted for 70% of usage, while e-books made up only 30%. However, the proportion of e-books rose steadily each year, surpassing printed books in 2021 and reaching 85% by 2023. Conversely, printed books declined to a mere 15%. This clear trend indicates a growing digitalization of reading habits on campus.

**常用句型模板**：
- **开头概述**：The chart/graph/diagram shows/illustrates the changes in… from… to…
- **数据对比**：While A accounted for X%, B made up only Y%.
- **趋势描述**：The figure for… rose/increased/declined/dropped steadily/gradually/sharply from… to…
- **转折强调**：However, a noticeable shift occurred in…
- **结尾总结**：This trend suggests that… is becoming more/less popular.
