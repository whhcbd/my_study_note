## 听力部分

**原文（VOA Learning English - AI in the Workplace）**

Artificial intelligence (AI) tools continue to expand in the workplace. And some U.S. states have launched specialized programs to train workers on how to deal with the new technologies.

Many industries are expected to heavily depend on a series of tools known as generative AI. This technology includes systems designed to perform many jobs currently done by humans. Such systems have become increasingly smarter in recent years.

In Connecticut, officials have been working to create what they hope will be the country's first Citizens AI Academy. This will be a free, online collection of special classes designed to teach basic AI skills and provide certifications needed for employment.

Connecticut Democratic Senator James Maroney told the Associated Press that AI changes in the workplace are happening very quickly. "So we need to all learn what are the best sources for staying current," he said. "How can we update our skills? Who can be trusted sources?"

Gregory LaBlanc is a professor of Finance, Strategy and Law at the Haas School of Business at Berkeley Law School in California. He says workers should be taught how to use and manage generative AI.

LaBlanc said he thinks it makes more sense to center on teaching ways to support AI "as opposed to learning to be really bad imitators of AI." He added that employees should be trained in areas where AI does not perform well so that workers can make up the difference.

This year, at least four states proposed legislation that sought ways to deal with AI in the classroom. Those states included Connecticut, California, Mississippi and Maryland.

One bill in California would require a state working group to consider including AI literacy skills in math, science, history and social science studies.

The writer of the bill is California Assemblyman Marc Berman. He said in a statement that AI offers the possibility to improve parts of our lives. "But only if we know how to use it, and use it responsibly," he added.

A study released by the job-search company Indeed found that all U.S. jobs listed on the service had skills that could be performed or improved by generative AI tools.

Nearly 20 percent of the Indeed job listings were considered "highly exposed." This means the technology is considered good or excellent at 80 percent of the skills the jobs require. Nearly 46 percent of jobs on the service were "moderately exposed." This means that AI tools can perform 50 to 80 percent of the skills.

## 阅读部分

**短文（约420词）**

### The Dual Nature of AI in Modern Employment

The integration of artificial intelligence into the workforce presents a paradoxical scenario that both excites and concerns economists, policymakers, and workers alike. On one hand, AI-driven automation promises unprecedented efficiency and productivity gains; on the other, it threatens to displace millions from traditional occupations.

Historically, technological revolutions have followed a predictable pattern: initial job displacement followed by the creation of new, often higher-skilled positions. The Industrial Revolution saw manual laborers replaced by machinery, but simultaneously created demand for engineers, factory managers, and maintenance technicians. Similarly, the Digital Revolution eliminated many clerical jobs while generating need for software developers, data analysts, and cybersecurity experts.

Contemporary AI systems, however, differ fundamentally from previous technologies in their capacity to perform cognitive tasks previously considered exclusive to human intelligence. Machine learning algorithms can now analyze medical images with accuracy surpassing radiologists, legal documents with speed exceeding paralegals, and financial data with precision outperforming analysts. This cognitive automation extends AI's impact beyond manual labor to intellectual professions.

The displacement effect is particularly pronounced in routine-based occupations. Studies from the McKinsey Global Institute indicate that approximately 30% of work activities in 60% of occupations could be automated using current technologies. Customer service representatives, data entry clerks, and telemarketers face the highest automation potential in the short term.

Conversely, AI creates demand for novel skill sets. Prompt engineering—the art of crafting precise instructions for AI systems—has emerged as a lucrative specialization. AI ethics consultants help organizations navigate the moral implications of automated decision-making. Meanwhile, human-AI collaboration specialists design workflows that leverage the complementary strengths of both.

The educational imperative is clear: workforce adaptation requires substantial investment in retraining programs. Countries like Finland and Singapore have implemented nationwide AI literacy initiatives, recognizing that technological proficiency must become a fundamental civic competency. Corporate training programs increasingly incorporate "future-proofing" modules that emphasize creativity, critical thinking, and emotional intelligence—capacities where humans retain comparative advantage.

Ultimately, the AI employment paradox may resolve not through job replacement but job redefinition. As economist David Autor observes, "Technology doesn't eliminate work; it reconfigures it." The challenge lies in ensuring this reconfiguration benefits all segments of society, not just the technologically elite.

## 翻译部分

**中文句子**：随着人工智能技术在各行各业的广泛应用，如何平衡技术创新与就业稳定已成为各国政府面临的重要挑战。

**参考译文**：With the widespread application of artificial intelligence technology across various industries, how to balance technological innovation with employment stability has become a significant challenge for governments worldwide.

**核心词汇解析**

1. **广泛应用** - widespread application
   - widespread: 广泛的，普遍的
   - application: 应用，运用
   
2. **各行各业** - across various industries / various sectors
   - industry: 行业，产业
   - sector: 部门，领域
   
3. **平衡...与...** - balance...with...
   - balance: 平衡，协调
   
4. **技术创新** - technological innovation
   - innovation: 创新，革新
   
5. **就业稳定** - employment stability
   - stability: 稳定，稳固
   
6. **面临的重要挑战** - a significant challenge facing... / has become a significant challenge for

**翻译技巧**
- 长句拆分：中文长句可拆分为英文的主从句结构
- 词性转换：中文动词"应用"转换为英文名词"application"
- 逻辑关系：使用with引导的介词短语表达伴随状况

## 写作片段

**写作主题**：Discuss both the potential benefits and challenges of artificial intelligence in reshaping the future job market. Write a paragraph of approximately 120 words expressing your viewpoint.

**范文**：

The integration of artificial intelligence into the labor market presents a complex landscape of opportunities and risks. On the positive side, AI can eliminate repetitive tasks, allowing workers to focus on creative and strategic activities that add greater value. It also creates entirely new professions in fields like AI ethics, prompt engineering, and machine learning operations. However, significant challenges accompany these benefits. Workforce displacement, particularly among those in routine-based occupations, requires comprehensive retraining programs and social safety nets. Moreover, the rapid pace of technological change may exacerbate existing inequalities if access to AI education remains uneven. Ultimately, successful adaptation demands proactive policies that foster lifelong learning while ensuring the benefits of automation are broadly shared across society.

**句型模板**

1. **表达双重性**：
   - X presents a complex landscape of opportunities and risks.
   - The integration of X brings both promising prospects and formidable challenges.
   
2. **列举优点**：
   - On the positive side, X can... allowing...
   - One significant advantage is that X enables...
   
3. **提出挑战**：
   - However, significant challenges accompany these benefits.
   - Despite these advantages, X raises concerns about...
   
4. **解决方案**：
   - Ultimately, successful adaptation demands...
   - Addressing these issues requires...

**写作技巧**
- 使用连接词：however, moreover, ultimately等使逻辑更清晰
- 平衡观点：同时讨论积极和消极方面
- 具体例证：提到具体职业如AI ethics consultant增加说服力
