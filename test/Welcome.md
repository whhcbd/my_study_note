Nowadays more and more college students have come to realize social practice and academic learning are equally important. Many job ads now ask for one to three years of work experience, so students know they need real practice to get a good job.

Why is practice so important? To begin with, book knowledge is not enough. Some students get high marks but still find it hard to solve real problems at work. This is because what we learn in class is sometimes different from what companies actually use. Second, working in a team and dealing with real projects is much harder than pushing on projects in the lab. We also need to learn how to communicate with colleagues and handle pressure from the senior.

In short, students should not only study hard but also take part in internships or social practice. By combining book knowledge with real experience, they can grow into capable graduates and stand out in the job market.
